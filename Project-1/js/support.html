Frequently Asked Questions

How do I properly care for my Métis sash?

Your sash should be hand-washed in cool water with gentle soap when necessary. Avoid wringing or twisting. Lay flat to dry away from direct sunlight or heat. Store folded or loosely rolled in a dry location. With proper care, your sash will last for generations.

What do the colors and patterns signify?

Traditional Métis sashes feature red, blue, green, yellow, white, and black in various combinations. While colors do not denote specific meanings, certain combinations are associated with different regions and weaving families. The arrow or chevron pattern is the distinctive mark of authentic L'Assomption finger-weaving.

How long does it take to create one sash?

A master weaver requires between 200 and 250 hours to complete a single Ceinture Fléchée. The entire process is done by hand using only the fingers to manipulate the threads. This extraordinary time investment ensures the exceptional quality and durability of each piece

Can I order a custom color combination?

Yes, we accept special orders for custom color combinations. Please contact our office with your specifications. Custom orders require a deposit and extended completion time of six to eight months, as each sash is woven specifically for the customer.
